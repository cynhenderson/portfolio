Japan Shopping List

Kappabashi Kitchenware

World-Class Blades

Japanese Kitchen Knives (Hōchō)

Kappabashi is famous for its world-class, exceptionally sharp blades. You can find everything from all-purpose chef's knives to specialty vegetable cleavers. Many stores offer tax-free shopping and custom engraving.

Elegant Designs

High-Quality Chopsticks (Hashi) & Rests

You will find entire stores dedicated to chopsticks, ranging from simple bamboo to elegant lacquered designs. You can also find cute, fanciful chopstick rests, such as animal shapes, to match.

Affordable Ceramics

Beautiful Japanese Tableware (Shokki)

The street is a treasure trove of affordable ceramics. Look out for lightweight teacups, beautifully painted plates, and sturdy ramen bowls.

Quirky Souvenirs

Realistic Fake Food Samples (Shokuhin Sanpuru)

These hyper-realistic plastic food models are a staple of Japanese restaurant displays and make for incredibly fun, quirky souvenirs. You can find them as magnets, keychains, and even phone cases.

Mess-Free Prep

Traditional Graters

Japanese graters often come with built-in trays to neatly collect ingredients like ginger, daikon radish, or wasabi without the mess.

For Sore Muscles

Roihi-Tsuboko Pain Patches

Famous "coin" patches that provide targeted heat for walking-induced aches.

Cult Favorite

V Rohto Premium Eye Drops

Advanced formula that refreshes tired eyes with a signature cooling sensation.

Post-Walking Relief

Kyusoku Jikan Cooling Leg Patches

Adheres easily to calves and feet, providing a soothing, cooling sensation to relieve tired, swollen legs after a long day of sightseeing.

Deep Moisture

Yuskin A Hand & Body Cream

A deeply moisturizing, vitamin-enriched cream that is a staple in Japanese households for healing dry hands, cracked heels, and winter skin.

Gentle Heat

Kairo (Stick-on Body Warmers)

These single-use heat packs can be stuck to the outside of a base layer shirt. They are perfect for keeping the lower back or shoulders comfortable and warm while out and about.

Onsen at Home

Japanese Bath Salts (Nyuyokuzai)

Back in the pharmacy section, look for boxes of bath salts like Kao Bub (carbonated tablets) or Bath Roman. They are formulated to mimic the mineral profiles of famous Japanese hot springs (onsen) and are absolute magic for soothing tired joints after a long day of walking and shopping.

Stationery, Home & Pantry

Best Ink

Uni-ball ONE pens

Known for having the darkest, most smudge-proof ink.

Smooth Writing

Zebra Sarasa Clip Gel Pens

These are a holy grail for smooth writing and are extremely popular in Japan. They are highly affordable there compared to stateside prices.

Heritage Item

Traveler's Notebook

A beautiful leather journal with swappable paper refills.

Bleed-Resistant

Kokuyo Campus or Soft Ring Notebooks

Japanese paper is famous for its smooth feel and high resistance to ink bleeding. The Kokuyo notebooks are lightweight, incredibly affordable, and a staple for organizing notes.

Gold Standard

Tombow Mono Eraser

This is considered the gold standard of erasers in Japan. It erases cleanly with minimal dust, gives precise control without damaging the paper underneath, and costs pocket change.

Office Engineering

Kokuyo Harinacs Staple-Free Stapler

A brilliant piece of Japanese office engineering that binds papers together by crimping the pages. It means you never have to buy or refill staples again.

Unrivaled Quality

Tabio Socks

The gold standard of hosiery. Incredible fit and durability.

Featherlight

Wpc. Tiny Umbrellas

World-class UV protection in a frame that weighs less than a smartphone.

Perfect Gift

Yoku Moku Cigare Cookies

Light, buttery rolled cookies. The beautiful tins make great souvenirs.

Affordable Luxury

Designer Handkerchiefs

On the first floor of almost any major Japanese department store (like Takashimaya, Mitsukoshi, or Isetan), you will find massive displays of beautifully packaged, licensed designer handkerchiefs. You can find brands like Ralph Lauren, Anna Sui, and Vivienne Westwood for around 1,000 to 1,500 yen ($7–$10 USD). They are elegant, practical, take up zero luggage space, and feel like a luxury purchase.

Restaurant Quality

Amano Foods Freeze-Dried Miso Soup

Skip the powdered packets. These are individual freeze-dried blocks found in supermarkets that bloom into rich, restaurant-quality soup with tofu, eggplant, or mushrooms that actually taste fresh. They are very inexpensive and make great lightweight gifts.

100-Yen Gem

High-Quality Laundry Nets

Japanese 100-yen shops (like Daiso, Seria, or Can Do) have the best laundry wash nets in the world. They are incredibly durable and come in specific, structured shapes for sweaters, delicates, and even shoes.

Pantry Upgrade

Unique Furikake (Rice Seasonings)

For just a couple of dollars, these colorful packets from the grocery store will instantly elevate plain rice, eggs, or popcorn. Look for fun, savory flavors like sukiyaki, butter soy sauce, or plum and shiso.
